More about me

Brazilian Jiu-jitsu (BJJ) is a self-defense martial art and combat sport based on grappling, ground fighting and submission holds. BJJ focuses on taking an opponent to the ground, gaining a dominant position, and using various techniques to force them into submission.

The word Jiu-Jitsu most literally translates to gentle art. The term gentle here has always been interesting to me because it is being used to describe a system where the intent is to gain control over someone else. Initially, this concept of gentle control fascinated me because I thought it might be helpful in different aspects of my life. Specifically, in getting others to see me for who I actually am and not just a 6 3, black man.

As I have continued my journey through this practice I have started to learn where gentleness comes into play. First, I had to learn how to be gentle with myself in the spaces where I am insecure by controlling my mindset. Then I had to learn how to be gentle with others while simultaneously setting clear boundaries for them to respect. Ultimately, I have learned that this art form is more about the collision of personal boundaries and overcoming fears and insecurities, all while remaining calm and maintaining control over the situation. I think Jiu-Jitsu is called the "gentle art" because it's the gentlest way to learn how to react to being in a vulnerable state and gain the tools to be able to defend yourself physically and mentally.
